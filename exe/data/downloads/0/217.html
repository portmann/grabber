Chase Freedom Unlimited Review: Is It Good?

By Cynthia Cohen

Editorial Note: This content is not provided by the card issuer. Opinions expressed here are author's alone, not those of the issuer, and have not been reviewed, approved or otherwise endorsed by the issuer.

Bottom Line

Great cash back credit card for everyday purchases

Earning cash back is a fantastic way for a credit card company to get your attention, but what other benefits are going to push it to the top of your application pile? With its juicy cash sign-up bonus, no annual fee, and introductory APR, the Chase Freedom Unlimited card separates itself from the pack by offering a higher-than-average cash back percentage every time you make a purchase.

But it's not right for everyone.

First, let’s take a look at what will spark your interest in the card.

10 REASONS TO GET THE CARD

  1. $150 Bonus: Earn a $150 Bonus after you spend $500 on purchases in your first 3 months from account opening. Unlike many cards that require you to spend thousands of dollars in the first three months of opening your account to get their bonus, you only need to shell out $500 during the first 3 months with the Chase Freedom Unlimited card and you’ll be served up a healthy 15,000 in rewards points. Those points can then be converted into $150 in cash rewards back to you (at a rate of 1 cent per point).

  2. You can get an additional $25 bonus: Have a spouse, loved one, or someone you’re willing to go to the mat for financially? Add them as an authorized user and pocket another $25 as a thank-you bonus when a purchase is made in the first 3 months.

  3. You’ll earn unlimited 1.5% cash back on every purchase: NEW! Unlimited 1.5% cash back on every purchase - it's automatic! With a flat 1.5% rewards rate, you’ll want to use the Chase Freedom Unlimited card for most — if not all — of your purchases (provided you pay off the balance each month). You’ll see how quickly you can rake in the rewards without having to enroll in any bonus categories.

  4. You don’t have to pay an annual fee: Always a plus.

  5. You can pool your points with Chase Sapphire Preferred or Ink Plus: Like to take vacations, or need to go on business trips? Well, the Chase Freedom Unlimited card isn’t a travel rewards card per se, but you can still capitalize on your trips by pooling your rewards points with your Chase Sapphire Preferred or Ink Plus credit card.

    Here’s how it works: Instead of redeeming your points for cash, you can pool your points with your Chase Sapphire Preferred or Ink Plus points. Then you can either transfer the points 1:1 to a travel partner program (such as Hyatt Hotels and United Airlines) or get 20% off travel when you redeem points for airfare, hotels, and car rentals through Chase Ultimate Rewards.

  6. You get a break with an introductory APR: If you need to make a large purchase soon and can't pay off the balance in full, the intro APR can help save you money during the promotional period. This card offers 0% Intro APR for 15 months from account opening on purchases and balance transfers, then a variable APR of 14.24-23.24%. Balance transfer fee is 5% of the amount transferred, $5 minimum.

  7. You won’t have to wait for your rewards: If it takes you a while before you hit a required benchmark, such as $25 or $50 in rewards, you’ll be happy to know that there’s no minimum amount required to redeem your cash back with the Freedom Unlimited.

  8. Your cash back rewards do not expire as long as your account is open: If you forget to redeem your rewards, you can breathe a sigh of relief because they’ll never expire as long as you keep the account open and it stays in good standing. So when you do notice those points you’ve racked up, you can rejoice knowing they’re still yours to convert into cash, redeem for gift cards, or combine with your Chase Sapphire Preferred or Ink Plus account for optimum travel rewards.

  9. You’re bargain-hunting habit will get rewarded: Everyone wants to save a buck, and if you scour ads (in print or online) to find a better price on eligible items you bought with the Chase Freedom Unlimited card, you will. With the Price Protection feature, you’ll have 90 days after purchasing your merchandise in the U.S. to find a lower advertised price. If you do, you can be reimbursed the difference for up to $500 per eligible item (the reimbursement maxes out at $2,500 per year).

  10. You only have to carry one card: Even in this day and age, not all cards are accepted everywhere (we’re talking to you, Amex and Discover). But the Chase Freedom Unlimited card offers widespread acceptance, which will serve you well if this is the credit card you choose to use. Plus, you get peace of mind, as you’re protected with $0 fraud liability, chip-enabled security, purchase protection, and auto rental collision damage waiver.

REASONS TO CONSIDER OTHER CARDS

  • You’ll pay if you travel overseas: A foreign transaction fee of 3% of each transaction in U.S. dollars is added every time you make a purchase in another country. You’d do much better to use a card that has no foreign transaction fee if you travel frequently.

  • There’s a hefty balance transfer fee: While many credit cards only charge a 3% balance transfer fee, you’ll pay either $5 or 5% of the amount of each transfer, whichever is greater with this card. So you may want to consider a card like the Chase Slate with its no balance transfer fee promotion instead.

  • You’ll never earn more than 1.5% back on your purchases: Chase has another card, the Chase Freedom, that will give you 5% on certain types of purchases that change every three months (they’re called rotating categories). Discover has one too, Discover It. With those cards, you have the potential to earn more than the Chase Freedom Unlimited if your spending falls in line with their bonus categories or if you are willing to think carefully about maximizing your rewards every time you spend.

  • You may be better off with a travel rewards card: If you travel but don't want to play the airline miles and points game, consider the Barclaycard Arrival Plus, which earns 2x miles on every purchase, plus you’ll get another 5% in miles back every time you redeem. But be aware — there’s also an $89 annual fee.

1.5% REWARDS

© Global Panorama (CC BY-SA 2.0) via Flickr

All day, all night, all the time. No matter what you purchase, you’ll garner unlimited 1.5% cash back. Unlike many other cards that only offer 1% on everyday purchases, if you do the math, you can see the advantages.

If you spend, on average, $2,500 per month on your card (which is easily possible if you pay bills with it), at 1% cash back you’ll earn $25.

If you use the Chase Freedom Unlimited card instead, and you’ll pocket $37.50 each month. At the end of the year, your wallet will be $150 fatter.

HOW IT COMPARES

You might want to consider these other popular rewards cards if you’re not sure the Chase Freedom Unlimited card works for your lifestyle:

Chase Freedom: Same family, separated by a word (“Unlimited”) and reward percentages. The Chase Freedom card offers only 1% back (vs. Chase Freedom Unlimited’s 1.5%), but the bigger difference is with the Freedom you get rotating bonus categories that offer 5% back when you make a purchase in that category, with a $1,500 per quarter limit. So if you think earning another 3.5% back on specific quarterly purchases is more beneficial, you might want to opt for the Chase Freedom card instead.

BOTTOM LINE

© Moyan Brenn (CC BY 2.0) via Flickr

The Chase Freedom Unlimited card is a very solid, strong cash back card. Whether you’re just making everyday purchases or paying bills, the cash back you’ll accrue, plus the long introductory APR period, is worth it.

And if you have the Chase Sapphire Preferred, then you must seriously consider getting this card, as the travel perks you’ll acquire by pooling your points from both cards is off the charts. You can then transfer them to airline travel partners, making the Chase Freedom Unlimited card one of the best no annual fee rewards credit cards we've seen.
