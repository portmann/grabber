The Sprinkler System is Broken

Posted by jefferson in Saving Money | 3 comments

The annual battle to determine who can have the greenest patch of grass in front of their house is a part of living in suburban America. In the house that we lived in a few years ago, we really didn’t stand much of a chance. We were surrounded by retirees who treated their yard like it was their baby. As a busy family of five, we just didn’t have the time required to keep things the grass appropriately watered, and keep the weeds at bay.

When we bought our current house, however, it came with a secret weapon, the sprinkler system! All we had to do was to setup a program into the panel in our garage, and the system would kick in every evening and water all of the “zones” around our yard for a few minutes. Our first summer in this house ended up being quite hot and dry, and we used the sprinklers pretty heavily. Sure enough, our yard stayed a lovely shade of emerald green, even as many of the neighbors grass turned brown. Haha! Take that!  But as it turned out, they had the last laugh, as our monthly water bill that summer was north of $400! (ouch)

The next summer, we did go ahead and open up the sprinkler system for the summer, but only used it sparingly. Frankly, we just didn’t have the money in the bank required to run a system of this kind. It turns out that Kermit the Frog was right; it ain’t easy being green. Near the end of the season, I noticed that the back-flow portion of the sprinkler had developed a small leak. Water was dripping out of the unit at a very slow pace, but it was clear that it was going to likely need some repairs if we decided to activate the system again.

Fast forward to this summer, and again we’ve entered the hot and dry portion of the midwest summer. When I turned the water on to the back-flow, the “small leak” had turned into a large “spray”. It was clear that the system was going to need a professional to take a look. I contacted one of the local sprinkler services and had them come out to assess the damage. It only took him a few minutes to quickly determine that my worst fears had come to fruition. The entire back-flow/pressure-valve portion of the system would need to be replaced. While much cheaper than installing a full system, the repairs would still cost over $700.

Paying $700 to repair the system was not a real option for us. Summer is always a difficult time in our family budget, with the combination of camp fees, electric bills that have been enhanced by air conditioning, and planning for a small family trip. While we did have enough to cover the repair in our emergency fund, we really couldn’t consider brown grass to be a true emergency. Instead, we had to come up with another solution.

Enter the cheap-o $10 sprinkler.

We actually ended up purchasing two of these bad boys, one for the front yard and one for the back yard. Of course, they don’t magically come on at the same time each day, and are not perfectly designed to perfectly cover every inch of every zone in our yard. Instead, we have to drag them out ourselves and try to place them in strategic spots where they get the most coverage. After a few minutes, we have to run out and reposition them somewhere else, while trying to avoid getting sprayed in the face. While the automated system runs every day regardless of how busy we are, our new system only runs when we have time. But all and all, its a big net win for us. Instead of paying over $700, we paid $20. I consider that a victory.

    A couple things you may already know:

    1. Make sure you only water in the evenings. Otherwise you’ll lose a LOT to evaporation.
